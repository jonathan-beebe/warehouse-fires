# 2026-04-10/11 — College Point, Queens, NY: Lumberyard Warehouse

**Status:** Cause under investigation

**Location:** College Point neighborhood, Queens, New York City

> **Note on date:** The fire began the evening of April 10, 2026 (per ABC7 NY, QNS, and CBS NY). It may have still been burning into April 11, which is the date the user noted. Both dates are plausible references to this incident.

## The Fire

A five-alarm fire broke out at a 64,000 sq ft industrial warehouse containing a lumberyard in the College Point neighborhood of Queens. FDNY received the call at 7:30 p.m. The fifth alarm brought:
- ~300 firefighters and EMS personnel
- 90 units
- 8 tower ladders deployed surrounding the structure

The building was declared a total loss. No injuries reported.

---

## Investigation

FDNY fire marshals are actively investigating the cause. As of the time of reporting, no arson determination has been made and no labor/wage connection has been identified.

> Note: This fire is distinct from the Flushing, Queens fire on March 16 (132-05 Avery Ave), which was a confirmed arson at a residential building. College Point and Flushing are different Queens neighborhoods.

---

## Context: Part of April 2026 Warehouse Fire Cluster

This was the fourth fire in a cluster of six significant warehouse fires across the United States in five days (April 7–11, 2026):
- April 7: Kimberly-Clark Distribution Center, Ontario, CA (confirmed arson — wage motive)
- April 8: Amazon, West Jefferson, OH (solar, not arson)
- April 9: Chemical warehouse, Newark, NJ (under investigation)
- **April 10: Lumberyard warehouse, College Point, Queens, NY (this incident)**
