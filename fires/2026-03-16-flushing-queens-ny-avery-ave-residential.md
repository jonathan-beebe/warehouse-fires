# 2026-03-16 — Flushing, Queens, NY: Avery Ave Residential Building Fire

**Status:** CONFIRMED ARSON — Murder & arson charges filed  
**Labor/Wage Connection:** INDIRECT — motive was rage over being fired (job loss), not wage protest  
**Date/Time:** March 16, 2026 (morning)  
**Location:** 132-05 Avery Ave, Flushing, Queens, New York City

> **Note:** This is a residential building fire, not a warehouse, but is included here because the confirmed arson motive was employment-related (fired from job). Four people died. It occurred weeks before the broader April 2026 warehouse fire cluster.

---

## The Fire

Roman Amatitla, 38, of Maspeth, Queens, allegedly set fire to a three-story residential building in Flushing after being fired from his job. He went to a nearby gas station, bought matches and a beer (stealing another), then went to the building and lit a piece of paper on fire, placing it on top of garbage near a stairwell.

He then reportedly sat on the curb sipping the stolen beer and watched as victims jumped from windows.

The building had pre-existing safety violations: trash-strewn hallways, leaks, debris-blocked stairways, and illegal subdivisions.

## Suspect

**Name:** Roman Amatitla, 38, of Maspeth, Queens  
**Motive (per prosecutors):** Was fired from his food processing job the day of the fire after getting into a fight at work. Told police he "needed to get his anger out."

---

## Charges

- 8 counts of second-degree murder
- First-degree arson
- Multiple assault charges

**Sentence exposure:** 25 years to life in prison if convicted.

---

## Labor Connection

The motive is employment-related but not a wage protest — Amatitla was fired and acted out of personal rage. This contrasts with the Kimberly-Clark case (April 7, 2026), where the suspect explicitly framed the act as protest against unlivable wages. The Flushing fire was prosecuted as mass murder.
