Warehouse fires are not new. The United States has averaged more than 1,000 warehouse fires every year for the past decade — roughly 1,500 to 1,700 annually in recent years, down from a peak of nearly 4,700 in 1980 as sprinkler codes improved. Most are accidental: electrical faults, heating equipment, operator error. What changed in April 2026 was not the frequency of fires but the context in which people are reading them: the Kimberly-Clark arson in Ontario, CA — where a worker filmed himself setting a 1.2-million-square-foot distribution center ablaze and explicitly cited wages — reframed an ordinary statistical backdrop as potential evidence of a labor uprising. This project exists to hold that line between what is confirmed and what is projection. See the historical baseline statistics for full data and source citations.
