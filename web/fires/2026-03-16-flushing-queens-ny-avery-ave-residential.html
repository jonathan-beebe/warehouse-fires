Note: This is a residential building fire, not a warehouse, but is documented here because the confirmed arson motive was employment-related (fired from job). It occurred weeks before the broader April 2026 warehouse fire cluster and is the only other confirmed arson in the dataset.

The Fire

Roman Amatitla, 38, of Maspeth, Queens, allegedly set fire to a three-story residential building in Flushing after being fired from his job. He went to a nearby gas station, bought matches and a beer (stealing another), then went to the building and lit a piece of paper on fire, placing it on top of garbage near a stairwell.

He then reportedly sat on the curb sipping the stolen beer and watched as victims jumped from windows.

The building had pre-existing safety violations: trash-strewn hallways, leaks, debris-blocked stairways, and illegal subdivisions.

Suspect

Roman Amatitla, 38, of Maspeth, Queens. Motive (per prosecutors): Amatitla was fired from his food processing job the day of the fire after getting into a fight at work. He told police he "needed to get his anger out."

Charges

  • 8 counts of second-degree murder
  • First-degree arson
  • Multiple assault charges

Sentence exposure: 25 years to life in prison if convicted.

Labor Connection

The motive is employment-related but not a wage protest. Amatitla was fired and acted out of personal rage, targeting a residential building rather than a workplace. This contrasts sharply with the April 7, 2026 Kimberly-Clark case, where the suspect explicitly framed the act as protest against unlivable wages. The Queens DA called this "an act of mass murder."
