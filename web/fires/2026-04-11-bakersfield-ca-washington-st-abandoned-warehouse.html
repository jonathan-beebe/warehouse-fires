The Fire

Multiple fire crews from the Kern County Fire Department and the Bakersfield Fire Department responded to a fire at an abandoned warehouse at 99 Washington Street in East Bakersfield. The warehouse was approximately 30,000 square feet and was filled with plastic materials in large bales stacked floor to ceiling. The heat was sufficient to buckle and collapse the building's metal framework.

The fire spread to the neighboring SA Recycling (scrap metal yard) facility before being contained. Eyewitness social media posts described it as "a warehouse right next to SA Recycling that then jumped to SA Recycling."

Property History

This is the third fire at 99 Washington Street, following two previous blazes at the same address in 2024. The repeat-fire history makes the site notable, but no arson determination has been made public for any of the three incidents.

Investigation

Cause under investigation. No arson determination or labor connection has been publicly reported. The repeat-fire nature of the site is notable and is mentioned in local news coverage.

Context: April 2026 Cluster

This was the fifth fire in a cluster of six significant warehouse fires across the United States in five days (April 7–11, 2026). It was widely circulated on social media as part of an apparent wave — the cause remains unknown.
