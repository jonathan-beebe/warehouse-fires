The Fire

A three-alarm fire broke out at a chemical warehouse at 104 Lister Avenue in the Ironbound neighborhood of Newark. Crews were called at 12:53 p.m. and the fire was upgraded from two alarms to three within the hour.

More than 100 members of the Newark Fire Department responded, along with the New Jersey EMS Task Force and the New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection (due to chemical contents). Seven ladder trucks were raised simultaneously at the height of operations. A drone was deployed to locate and extinguish remaining hotspots. All 21 warehouse employees escaped safely; no injuries were reported.

Investigation Status

The cause of the fire is under investigation. No arson determination has been made public. No labor or wage connection has been reported.

Context: April 2026 Cluster

This fire was the third in a cluster of six significant warehouse fires across the United States in five days (April 7–11, 2026), following the Kimberly-Clark arson in Ontario, CA and the Amazon solar panel fire in West Jefferson, OH.
