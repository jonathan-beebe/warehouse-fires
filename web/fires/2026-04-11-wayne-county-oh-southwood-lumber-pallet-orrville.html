Date note: Listed in some sources as April 11, others as April 12. This is the same fire — it started Saturday evening April 11 and crews remained through early morning April 12. Both dates refer to this single incident.

The Fire

A five-alarm fire (later upgraded to seven alarms for water supply assistance) broke out at Southwood Lumber Pallet Inc., a pallet and lumber business that has operated for 42 years. The initial dispatch came from Apple Creek Volunteer Fire Department just before 8 p.m.

The roof of the main building collapsed shortly after crews arrived. A total of 24 fire departments responded with over 75 firefighters, more than 30 apparatus, and more than 500,000 gallons of water used to fight the blaze. East Lincoln Way was temporarily closed. Crews remained on scene until approximately 1 a.m.

Investigation

The cause of the fire is under investigation by the Ohio State Fire Marshal's Office. No arson determination has been made and no labor/wage connection has been reported.

Business Impact

Southwood Lumber Pallet, in business for 42 years, stated they are "grateful no one was injured" and plan to rebuild. The business is working with investigators and their insurance carrier on recovery. Community members have rallied to support the business following the fire.
