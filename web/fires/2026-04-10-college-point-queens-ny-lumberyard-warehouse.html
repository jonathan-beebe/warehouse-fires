Date note: The fire began the evening of April 10, 2026 (per ABC7 NY, QNS, and CBS NY). It may have still been burning into April 11, which is why both dates appear in social media coverage of the cluster.

The Fire

A five-alarm fire broke out at a 64,000 sq ft industrial warehouse containing a lumberyard in the College Point neighborhood of Queens. FDNY received the call at 7:30 p.m. The fifth alarm brought approximately 300 firefighters and EMS personnel, 90 units, and 8 tower ladders deployed surrounding the structure. The building was declared a total loss. No injuries were reported.

Investigation

FDNY fire marshals are actively investigating the cause. As of the time of reporting, no arson determination has been made and no labor/wage connection has been identified.

This fire is distinct from the Flushing, Queens fire on March 16 (132-05 Avery Ave), which was a confirmed arson at a residential building. College Point and Flushing are different Queens neighborhoods.

Context: April 2026 Cluster

This was the fourth fire in a cluster of six significant warehouse fires across the United States in five days (April 7–11, 2026). Social media frequently grouped it with intentional fires — no such determination has been made.
